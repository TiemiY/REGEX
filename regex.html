REGEX: Regular Expressions
REGEX is a pattern of characters that you can use to check a string. A sequence of characters that defines a search pattern. [1]
    Syntax Summary
  • ^regex: starts with
  • regex$: ends with
  • regex?: zero or 1 match {0,1}
  • regex*: zero or more matches {0,∞}
  • regex+: 1 or more matches {1,∞}
  • regex{number}: number of times
  • [regex]: provides a range construct.
  • (regex): groups patterns
  • [^regex]: "not" it will not seach for the pattern
    Shortcuts
  • [a-z]: all lowercase alphabet
  • [A-Z]: all uppercase alphabet
  • [:alpha:]: any letter (lowercase or uppercase)
  • [0-9] or \d or [:digit:]: all numbers
  • [a-zA-Z0-9] or \w: all lower and capital alphabet and numbers
  • \W: only special characters
  • \b: border, indicates the limit of the search
  • . : dot means any character
  • \ : back-slash means escape used in case of special characters
  • | : pipe means or
  • sed: it is necessary to escape some characters: {} | ()
  • [^] = "not"
